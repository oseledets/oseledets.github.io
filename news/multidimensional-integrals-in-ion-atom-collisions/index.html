Multidimensional integrals in ion-atom collisions

Posted on: 2014-03-19 00:00:00

Simulation in physics is full of multidimensional integrals. In the paper M. S. Litsarev and I. V. Oseledets. Fast multidimensional convolution in low-rank formats via cross approximation. arXiv preprint 1403.4068, 2014 we consider the DEPOSIT code that is suited for the computation of ion-atomic collisons. The main computational bottleneck is the computation of a three-dimensional integral. We exploit its structure by computing low-rank separated representation using cross approximation for a two-dimensional function, and exponential sums approach for the Slater density function. Implementation of this technique decreases the total computational time by a factor of 1000. What is more importance, the general concept can be applied to more complicated models (like ion-molecule collision).
